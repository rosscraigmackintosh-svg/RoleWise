# AI boundaries

Rolewise uses AI to analyse job descriptions, extract requirements, and surface patterns. These boundaries define where AI is permitted to operate and where human judgment must remain primary.

## Permitted AI tasks

- Parsing job descriptions to extract requirements, responsibilities, and signals
- Identifying patterns in language that indicate culture, expectations, or risk
- Summarising structured information already present in the source material
- Surfacing gaps between stated requirements and candidate background

## Prohibited AI tasks

- Predicting hiring outcomes or likelihood of success
- Generating numeric fit scores or ratings
- Making recommendations about whether a candidate should apply
- Drawing conclusions about a candidate's character, personality, or suitability beyond stated evidence
- Inferring protected characteristics or using them in analysis

## AI must show its reasoning

When the AI makes an assessment, it must surface the evidence it is drawing from. "You appear to lack X" must be paired with "because the JD states Y and your background does not include Z."

Conclusions must be grounded in visible evidence from the source material.

## Confidence calibration

The AI must not overstate certainty. When information is ambiguous, it must say so. Language like "this likely means", "this could indicate", or "this is unclear" is preferred over definitive statements.

## Human override

Any AI output can be corrected, dismissed, or overridden by you. Your assessment is authoritative. The AI is an input, not a decision-maker.

## In one sentence
AI supports analysis and surfaces patterns — it does not predict, score, or decide.

# Evidence principles

Rolewise is an evidence-based tool. This section defines what counts as evidence, how it is used, and where its limits are.

## What counts as evidence

Evidence in Rolewise must be:

- **Observable** — something that happened or was documented, not inferred from feeling
- **Specific** — tied to a particular role, interaction, or event, not a general impression
- **Durable** — recorded in a way that persists and can be reviewed over time

Examples of evidence:
- A job description with specific requirements stated
- A recruiter response with a stated timeline
- A rejection with a reason given (or notably absent)
- A stage reached or not reached in a hiring process

Examples of things that are not evidence:
- A sense that the recruiter "seemed interested"
- A gut feeling about fit
- An assumption about why no response was received

## How evidence is used

Evidence in Rolewise is used to:

- Identify gaps between candidate background and role requirements
- Track the state of active applications
- Surface patterns across the pipeline over time
- Support the weekly review process

Evidence is not used to:
- Generate scores or ratings
- Predict outcomes
- Make recommendations that bypass candidate judgment

## Evidence limits

Not all questions have evidence-based answers. When evidence is insufficient, Rolewise acknowledges that rather than generating answers from incomplete data.

See [Evidence limits](/docs/data-logic/evidence-limits) in the Data & Logic section for specific rules on where the product must stop.

## In one sentence
Evidence must be observable, specific, and durable — everything else is not evidence.

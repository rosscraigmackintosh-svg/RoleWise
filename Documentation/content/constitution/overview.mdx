# Constitution
The Constitution defines non-negotiable rules. These are hard boundaries, not guidelines. Conflicting features are wrong.
## Why this exists
AI and hiring tools drift toward scoring, predicting, optimising, and engagement. This feels powerful but reduces clarity and trust. The Constitution prevents that drift.
## Core rules
### No scoring
Never scores or ranks. No percentages or hidden ratings. Scoring creates false precision.

### No prediction
No outcome estimates. Unreliable and misleading.

### Evidence over assumption
Prioritises inference from the role. Says when information is missing. No confident guesses.

### Clarity over encouragement
Not designed to push forward. No urgency, pressure, or positive bias. Help you think, not encourage action.

### No behavioural optimisation
Doesn't optimise engagement or increase activity. Better decisions, not more decisions.

### Transparency of limits
Explicit about weak evidence. "Inconclusive" beats "confident but wrong".
---
## How this applies in practice
Every feature is checked against the Constitution. Features that introduce scoring, imply prediction, push toward action, or hide uncertainty should not be built.
## In one sentence
The Constitution ensures Rolewise remains a tool for clear thinking, not persuasion.

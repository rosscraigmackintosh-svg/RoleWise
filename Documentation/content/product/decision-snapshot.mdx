# Decision Snapshot
Decision Snapshot captures each decision you make about a role.
It records what the role was, how it was interpreted, and why you chose to apply, skip, or wait.
## Why it exists
Most job decisions disappear. You read a role, decide quickly, and move on. Without that context, patterns are hard to see. Decision Snapshot preserves your reasoning.
## What it does
For each role, Decision Snapshot stores:
- a structured view of the role (Role DNA)
- key signals and potential blockers
- your decision (apply, skip, or watch)
- optional notes or context from you

A lightweight record of your thinking at the time.
## How it behaves
Decision Snapshot follows the same principles as the rest of Rolewise.
### Captures reasoning, not judgement
Reflects what you saw and interpreted. Doesn't evaluate correctness.

### Lightweight by design
Quick and natural, not a form to fill.

### Structured, not verbose
Structured fields make it easy to review, compare, and identify patterns later.

## What it is not
Decision Snapshot is not:
- a performance log
- a scoring system
- a detailed diary
- a justification engine

No long explanations or forced reflection.
## How it fits into the product
```
Applicant Mode → Decision → Snapshot → Weekly Review
```

Decision Snapshot sits between decision and reflection:
- Applicant Mode helps you understand the role
- You decide (apply, skip, or watch)
- Decision Snapshot records that moment
- Weekly Review connects those decisions over time

The link between individual choices and long-term understanding.
## Over time
As more snapshots are collected, they become more valuable.
They allow you to:
- revisit past decisions with context
- spot repeated patterns in what you accept or reject
- understand how your thinking is evolving

Make patterns visible when they matter, not analyse every decision.
## In one sentence
Decision Snapshot captures why you made a decision so you can understand it later.

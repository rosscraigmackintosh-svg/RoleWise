# Role DNA
Role DNA is the structured representation of a role inside Rolewise.
It translates a job description into a consistent set of traits that describe how the role actually behaves.
## Why it exists
Job descriptions are inconsistent. Similar-looking roles differ in scope, ownership, expectations, and working style. Role DNA creates a consistent way of describing roles.
## What it is
Role DNA is a set of structured traits extracted from a job description.
These traits describe key aspects of the role, such as:
- seniority and level of ownership
- scope and breadth of responsibility
- clarity vs ambiguity
- company stage and environment
- expectations around delivery, speed, and impact
- collaboration model and stakeholders

Each role is interpreted into this structure for clearer understanding.
## What it does
Role DNA makes it possible to:
- understand the shape of a role more quickly
- compare roles on a consistent basis
- identify potential mismatches or blockers
- recognise patterns across multiple roles

A stable foundation for decision-making.
## How it behaves
Role DNA follows the same principles as the rest of Rolewise.
### Descriptive, not definitive
Informed interpretation, not perfect.

### Honest about uncertainty
Reflects lack of clarity. No confident assumption-filling.

### Consistent across roles
Same structure allows meaningful comparison.

### Supports, not replaces judgement
Helps you understand. Doesn't decide if it's right.
## What it is not
Role DNA is not:
- a score
- a ranking system
- a personality match
- a prediction of success

Doesn't reduce roles to numbers or verdicts.
## How it fits into the product
Role DNA underpins multiple parts of Rolewise:
- Applicant Mode uses it to explain the role
- Decision Snapshot stores it as part of your decision
- Weekly Review uses it to identify patterns
- Radar uses it to surface relevant roles

The shared structure connecting the system.
## Over time
As more roles are analysed, Role DNA becomes more useful.
It allows you to see:
- which types of roles you are drawn to
- which environments work for you
- where consistent friction appears

This is not done through scoring, but through repeated, structured interpretation.
## In one sentence
Role DNA is the structured way Rolewise understands and compares roles.

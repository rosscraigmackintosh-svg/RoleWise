# Weekly Review
Weekly Review is the reflection layer of Rolewise. It helps you step back and understand patterns across decisions, applications, and outcomes.
## Why it exists
Job searching is often reactive.
You read a role, make a quick decision, move on, and repeat. Over time, it becomes difficult to see patterns:
- which roles you are drawn to
- which ones you skip
- what leads to interviews
- what consistently doesn't work

Without reflection, decisions feel isolated. Weekly Review reconnects them.
## What it does
Weekly Review brings together your recent activity into a single view.
It helps you:
- see what you applied to, skipped, or ignored
- understand where time and attention went
- identify recurring blockers or friction
- recognise patterns across roles and outcomes
- reflect on whether your decisions are improving

It helps you observe, not evaluate.
## How it behaves
Weekly Review follows the same principles as the rest of Rolewise.
### Descriptive, not judgemental
Shows what happened. No performance scoring.

### Pattern-focused
Value from patterns, not single events.

### Calm and periodic
Intentionally slower and reflective. Space to think, not react.

### Honest about limits
Says when data is unclear. No forced conclusions.
## What it is not
Weekly Review is not:
- a performance dashboard
- a productivity tracker
- a gamified progress system
- a tool to optimise application volume

Doesn't turn your search into a metric.
## How it fits into the product
```
Roles → Decisions → Snapshots → Weekly Review
↓
Patterns
```

Weekly Review sits across the entire workflow:
- Radar surfaces roles
- Applicant Mode helps you decide
- Outcomes are logged over time
- Weekly Review connects everything

Where short-term actions become longer-term understanding.
## Over time
As more roles and outcomes are captured, Weekly Review becomes more useful.
It helps you answer questions like:
- Am I applying to the right kinds of roles?
- Are the roles I choose leading to meaningful conversations?
- Are there consistent blockers I should address?

The goal is not to optimise behaviour, but to improve judgement.
## In one sentence
Weekly Review helps you understand your decisions over time, not just one role at a time.

# Radar
Radar is the discovery layer of Rolewise.
It helps you stay aware of relevant roles over time without relying on high-volume searching or noisy job feeds.
## Why it exists
Most discovery tools optimise for volume, creating noise. Radar reduces that by shifting from constant searching and reactive browsing to structured awareness and selective attention.

## What it does
Radar surfaces roles intentionally, helping you stay aware, avoid noise, recognise patterns, and focus on worth evaluating. It sits alongside job boards, not replacing them.
## How it behaves
Radar follows the same principles as the rest of Rolewise.
### Signal over volume
Clarity over completeness. Fewer, clearer signals beat large volumes.

### Calm, not reactive
No urgency, notifications, or pressure to act fast. Awareness, not reaction.

### No ranking or scoring
Roles surface to support judgment, not hidden scores or engagement optimisation.

### Works with Applicant Mode
Radar surfaces, Applicant Mode helps understand. Discover → Analyse → Decide.

## What it is not
Radar is not:
- a job board
- a ranking engine
- a recommendation algorithm optimised for clicks
- a feed designed to keep you scrolling

Doesn't maximise activity.
## Over time
As more roles are analysed, Radar becomes more useful.
Patterns begin to emerge:
- common role types
- recurring requirements
- consistent blockers

These help you refine attention and focus.
## In one sentence
Radar helps you stay aware of relevant roles without being overwhelmed by noise.

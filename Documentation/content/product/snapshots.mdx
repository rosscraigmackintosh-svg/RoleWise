# Snapshots

A Snapshot is a point-in-time record of an application's state. It captures what is known about a role, an application, and its current position in the hiring process at a specific moment.

## Purpose

Applications change. What you know at application differs from what you know after first interview. Snapshots record the state at each significant moment, preserving history and making evolution visible.

## What a snapshot contains

- Current stage
- Date of snapshot
- Most recent recruiter interaction
- Known requirements and gaps (from initial analysis)
- Open questions still outstanding
- Assessment of momentum (active, stalling, unclear)
- Notes from the candidate

## When to create a snapshot

Snapshots are created at:

- Point of application
- After each recruiter or interviewer interaction
- When a stage transition occurs
- At the point of outcome (offer, rejection, withdrawal)

## Snapshots are not edited

Snapshots are fixed records. They're reviewed but never changed, preserving history accurately. If your understanding changes, create a new snapshot.

## Using snapshots in review

Snapshots provide role history for the Weekly Review. They show how your understanding evolved and whether the role delivered on initial signals.

## In one sentence
Snapshots preserve the state of an application at each significant moment so history remains accurate and reviewable.

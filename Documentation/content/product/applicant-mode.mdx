# Applicant Mode
Applicant Mode is the core decision layer of Rolewise. It helps you read a job description clearly and decide if it's worth pursuing.
## Why it exists
Job descriptions are often dense, repetitive, and unclear.
Important signals are buried, softened, or missing entirely. This makes it difficult to quickly understand:
- what the role actually involves
- what is expected
- where the risks are

Applicant Mode restructures that information into something easier to reason about.
## What it does
When a user provides a job description, Applicant Mode:
- extracts the core shape of the role
- identifies likely responsibilities and expectations
- surfaces potential blockers and risks
- highlights vague or missing information
- summarises the role in plain English

The goal is clarity, not judgment.
## The decision layer
Applicant Mode supports a simple decision: Apply, Skip, or Keep watching. It doesn't tell you what to do — it gives you a clearer basis for deciding.
## How it behaves
Applicant Mode follows these principles:
### Descriptive, not predictive
Describes what the role appears to be. No outcome predictions.

### Honest about uncertainty
Vague roles surface as vague. No confident assumptions filling gaps.

### Trade-offs are visible
Highlights scope vs clarity, ownership vs ambiguity, expectations vs support.

### Calm presentation
Easy to scan and think through. No urgency, pressure, or visual noise.

## How it fits into the product
```
Input: Job Description
↓
Applicant Mode Analysis
↓
Decision: Apply / Skip / Watch
```

Applicant Mode sits at the start of the workflow:
1. User provides a job description
2. Rolewise analyses the role
3. User decides whether to apply

From there, the role can move into tracking, follow-ups, and outcome logging.
## Over time
As you evaluate more roles, patterns emerge. Applicant Mode feeds this learning without scoring or ranking you.
## In one sentence
Applicant Mode helps you understand a role clearly before deciding whether to apply.

# Role DNA structure
Role DNA is the structured representation of a role.
It allows roles to be compared, tracked, and understood consistently.
## Core traits
Each role is broken into key dimensions:
- scope
- ownership
- clarity
- expectations
- constraints
## Supporting traits
Additional signals may include:
- company stage
- product type
- team structure (if known)
- delivery pressure
- ambiguity level
## Behaviour
### Consistent structure
Every role uses the same framework.
---
### Derived from signals
Traits come from the job description, not assumptions.
---
### Supports comparison
Roles can be compared without scoring.
## Why it matters
Without structure, roles remain vague.
Role DNA turns description into something usable.
## In one sentence
Role DNA turns a job description into a consistent, comparable structure.

# Data & Logic
This section explains how Rolewise interprets roles: what it uses, how it reasons, and where its limits are.
## Why this matters
Rolewise is a decision-support tool. Its behaviour must be understandable — you should see what it's based on, what it can infer, and where it's uncertain. Clarity of logic builds trust.
## What Rolewise uses
Rolewise primarily works from:
- job descriptions (text provided by the user)
- explicit signals within that text (responsibilities, requirements, expectations)
- structural patterns across roles (common role shapes and language patterns)

No hidden personal data or behavioural tracking.
## How roles are interpreted
```
Job Description
↓
Signal Extraction
↓
Role DNA (structured traits)
↓
Decision Support
```

A job description is translated into a structured representation (Role DNA).
This involves:
- identifying key signals (scope, ownership, expectations)
- recognising patterns in how roles are described
- grouping those signals into consistent traits

The result is a clearer, structured view of the role.
## What Rolewise can infer
From a job description, Rolewise can often infer:
- the likely level of ownership
- the expected scope of work
- how clearly the role is defined
- potential trade-offs (e.g. ambiguity vs structure)
- areas where expectations may be high or unclear

Reasoned interpretations, not exact truths.
## What Rolewise cannot know
Rolewise cannot reliably determine:
- actual company culture
- team quality or leadership style
- internal constraints or politics
- hiring intent behind the role
- real compensation flexibility

Treat them as unknowns when they matter.
## How uncertainty is handled
Uncertainty is not hidden. Vague roles surface as vague with limited conclusions and no confident claims. "Inconclusive" is a valid outcome.
## No scoring or prediction
Rolewise does not:
- score the role
- score the user
- predict hiring outcomes
- estimate success probability

They introduce false precision and reduce trust.
## Consistency of logic
All roles are interpreted using the same structure.
This consistency enables role comparison, pattern recognition, and clearer understanding of what works.

## In one sentence
Rolewise uses structured interpretation of job descriptions to support clearer decisions, while being explicit about what it does and does not know.

# Long-term direction
Rolewise starts with job description analysis, but it does not end there.
Its long-term direction is to become a broader decision system for role selection and hiring reality.
## Phase one
The first phase is individual role clarity.
Users bring in roles. Rolewise helps them interpret those roles, decide whether to apply, and preserve the reasoning behind that choice.
## Phase two
The second phase is pattern recognition.
Over time, repeated decisions and outcomes make it possible to show:
- recurring blockers
- common mismatch patterns
- types of roles that progress further
- low-signal rejection systems that should not be over-interpreted
## Phase three
The longer-term direction is a richer hiring reality layer.
This could include:
- better understanding of role types over time
- aggregate hiring friction patterns
- clearer market interpretation at the role level
- stronger user understanding of what to pursue and what to ignore
## What must not change
The long-term direction does not justify drifting into:
- scoring users
- ranking people
- predicting hiring outcomes
- behavioural manipulation
- recruiter-side filtering tools

Growth should add depth, not compromise the product's stance.
## Strategic rule
Every expansion should strengthen the core question:
> Is this role worth pursuing?

If it does not, it is probably a distraction.
## In one sentence
Rolewise should grow from single-role clarity into long-term role decision intelligence without abandoning its trust-first boundaries.

# Positioning
Rolewise is a decision-support product.
It helps people decide whether a role is worth pursuing before they apply. That positioning matters because it keeps the product clear, defensible, and distinct from the tools around it.
## What Rolewise is not competing to be
Rolewise is not trying to replace:
- job boards
- ATS platforms
- recruiter CRMs
- sourcing tools
- CV scoring products

Those products serve different jobs.
## Where Rolewise sits
Rolewise sits upstream of application.
It operates in the space between:
- discovering a role
- entering a hiring process

That moment is under-served. Most tools either flood the user with options or judge them after they have already committed. Rolewise focuses on the decision before that commitment.
## Core positioning line
Rolewise helps people decide whether a role is worth pursuing before they apply.
## Why that positioning is strong
It is clear.
It is useful.
It is ethically cleaner than scoring or prediction.
It creates room for long-term learning without becoming an ATS or a job board.
## How Rolewise should be understood
Rolewise should be understood as:
- a role interpretation layer
- a decision support system
- a reflection tool for role choices over time

It should not be framed as:
- a job search accelerator
- an AI career coach
- a hiring predictor
- a candidate ranking tool
## Positioning constraints
Any new feature should strengthen this positioning, not dilute it.
If a feature makes Rolewise feel more like a feed, funnel, or scoring engine, it is pulling in the wrong direction.
## In one sentence
Rolewise is positioned as a decision-support layer before application, not a tool for volume, prediction, or hiring automation.

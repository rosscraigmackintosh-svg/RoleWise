# What it is not
Rolewise is defined by what it doesn't do. These boundaries protect it from drifting toward activity, prediction, or false confidence.
## Not a job board
Rolewise doesn't surface more roles or increase volume. It helps you evaluate once you've found something.
## Not a scoring system
No percentages, match scores, or hidden ratings. Scoring creates false precision and overconfidence.
## Not a prediction tool
No outcome predictions. These signals mislead more than they inform.
## Not an optimisation engine
No behaviour optimisation. No urgency, nudges, or pressure. Better decisions, not throughput.
## Not a recruiter tool
Not built for companies to filter candidates. Exists on the candidate side to support better judgment.
## Why these boundaries matter
Most tools drift toward scoring, prediction, optimisation, and engagement loops. These make people feel confident but reduce decision quality. Rolewise avoids them deliberately — designed to be useful, not persuasive.
## In one sentence
Rolewise helps you decide, not decides for you.

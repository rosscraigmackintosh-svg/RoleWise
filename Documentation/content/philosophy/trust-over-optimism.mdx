# Trust over optimism

Most job search tools default to optimism: scoring candidates, predicting outcomes, and selling hope. They make people feel better. Rolewise does the opposite. The goal is not to feel good — it is to know clearly.

## The cost of optimism

A 78% match score for a role you're not qualified for does two things:

1. Encourages wasted applications
2. Trains you to interpret weak evidence favourably — compounding over time

The second is more damaging. Candidates who learn to inflate their own assessment become worse at evaluating fit in future searches.

## Trust requires honesty

Trust comes from being right, not encouraging. Rolewise surfaces information even when it's unwelcome. The product isn't an advocate — it's honest.

## What this looks like in practice

- A role that requires skills you do not have will be flagged as a gap, not softened as "development opportunity"
- A recruiter who stops responding after initial contact will be logged as a signal, not explained away
- A repeated pattern of rejections at the same stage will be surfaced, not hidden to avoid discouraging the user

These are uncomfortable things to present. They are also the things that are most useful.

## Optimism has its place

This is not against hope. Resilience and motivation must come from the candidate, not the product. Rolewise provides clear information and trusts you to make your own judgment.

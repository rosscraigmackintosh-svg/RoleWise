# Decision support, not prediction

A tool that helps you decide differs fundamentally from one that decides for you. Rolewise is the former.

## Why prediction is a trap

Hiring is not deterministic. Identical candidates get different outcomes because hiring involves human judgment calls under pressure with incomplete information. Any tool claiming to predict outcomes makes an unsupported claim — and worse, it trains candidates to defer to predictions instead of developing their own judgment.

## What decision support looks like

Decision support provides relevant, structured information and leaves the decision to you. In Rolewise:

- Job descriptions are broken into component parts for clear assessment
- Buried requirements surface
- You log what you know and don't know
- Pipeline tracking reveals patterns

None of these tell you what to do. All help you see more clearly.

## The judgment stays with you

Your judgment is the most important variable in a job search. Rolewise exists to improve it, not replace it.

Rolewise sometimes surfaces information you'll disagree with. You may have context it doesn't — relationships, domain knowledge, insight into an organisation. Rolewise is an input to your decision, not the decision itself.

## Designing for judgment

This shapes product design. Features that replace judgment are avoided. Features that sharpen it are built. The question is always: does this help you think more clearly, or does it think for you?

## In one sentence
Rolewise supports your judgment — it never replaces it.

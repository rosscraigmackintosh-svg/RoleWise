Stripe · London · Contract
Senior Product Manager
Product Squad PM · Payments & Growth
Role Summary

A senior individual contributor PM role focused on Stripe's payments product in London. Scope centres on merchant-facing product — improving conversion, reducing friction in checkout flows, and iterating on the core payments API experience.

This role exists because Stripe is expanding European PM capacity as merchant adoption grows in the region. The hire is likely to own a defined payment vertical rather than a generalist PM function, which is a positive scope signal.

Fit Reality
Strong product ownership expected — full roadmap accountability with direct stakeholder alignment, not execution-layer PM work.
"owns the end-to-end product roadmap for your domain, from ideation through delivery"
Technical depth required but scope is undefined — coding collaboration is mentioned without a clear boundary between PM and engineering ownership.
"partner closely with engineering on technical decisions and trade-offs"
Senior IC positioning is appropriate — no people management expectation stated, scope aligns with 6–8 years of experience, not a stretch.
"individual contributor role with significant cross-functional influence across the London team"
Fintech domain experience implied — payments-specific language throughout, though not explicitly required as a hard prerequisite.
"experience in payments, fintech, or complex transactional products preferred"
What You'd Do
Define and own the product roadmap for a defined payment product vertical from ideation through delivery.
"own the roadmap for your product area, prioritising ruthlessly based on merchant impact"
Lead cross-functional alignment across engineering, design, data science, legal, and compliance.
"working closely with engineering, design, and compliance to ship iteratively"
Conduct merchant research to identify friction points and surface actionable opportunity areas.
"gathering feedback from merchants and converting qualitative insights into clear requirements"
Drive measurable improvements to conversion-critical flows using a data-driven prioritisation process.
"improving conversion and reducing drop-off across key payment flows with measurable outcomes"
What They're Looking For
6+ years PM experience — ideally in fintech, payments, or a high-volume transactional product context.
"6+ years of product management experience, preferably in payments or high-volume B2B SaaS"
Comfort working alongside world-class engineers — ability to engage credibly on technical decisions without being the decision-maker.
"ability to collaborate with and influence a world-class engineering team on technical direction"
Strong analytical and data mindset — evidence of using quantitative data to drive product prioritisation decisions at scale.
"data-driven approach to prioritisation, comfortable with SQL and dashboarding tools"
Friction Signals
Coding scope undefined — "technical collaboration" is mentioned repeatedly but the JD never defines where PM ownership ends and engineering ownership begins. This needs resolving in the first call.
Hybrid days stated as a minimum, not a contract term — "3 days on-site minimum" appears once but is not restated in the contract terms section. Confirm before offer stage.
No reporting structure stated — unclear whether this role reports to a Group PM or directly to VP Product. Team size and squad composition are also omitted.
Questions to Ask
1
What does success look like at 30, 60, and 90 days in this role?
2
Where does PM ownership end and engineering ownership begin on technical product decisions day-to-day?
3
Is the 3-day hybrid requirement fixed week-to-week, or is there flexibility around travel and external commitments?
4
Who would this role report to, and what does that relationship look like in practice?
5
Is this a new role or a backfill, and if a backfill — what changed?
Monzo · London · Permanent
Product Lead
Product Squad PM · Consumer FinTech
Role Summary

A product leadership role with ambiguous IC vs management framing. Monzo's product culture is strong, but the "Lead" title is being used inconsistently — the JD blends individual contributor output expectations with team management responsibilities without separating them clearly.

Fit Reality
IC vs management scope genuinely unclear — the JD expects both deep IC product work and line management of 2–3 PMs. This is a stretch combination at most companies.
"will own a product domain while mentoring and managing a small team of product managers"
Salary is below London fintech median for this level — £95k for a lead PM with management responsibilities at a mature fintech is in the bottom quartile. Equity compensation is unconfirmed.
"competitive salary plus equity" — no range given
Genuine product culture signal — language across the JD indicates real product ownership rather than feature delivery. Monzo's PM function has a strong reputation.
Friction Signals
IC + management expectations combined — the role expects both deep IC product output and people management, without a clear senior PM counterpart to take IC load. Risky scope.
Salary below market for level — £95k at a Lead level with management responsibilities is below the £110–130k London fintech median for this scope.
Questions to Ask
1
Is the expectation that I'm primarily an IC who manages, or a manager who ships product work too?
2
What's the total compensation package including equity and benefits?
3
What does the hybrid pattern look like in practice — are the days fixed or flexible?

Lead UX Designer
Product Squad Designer · Consumer FinTech
Role Summary

Applied — currently in Round 2. A fully remote design lead role with genuine product ownership across a defined consumer vertical. Revolut's design function has matured significantly over the past 18 months and this role sits in a team with real UX influence.

Fit Reality
Real design ownership for a specific vertical — not an execution-layer design role; you'd own end-to-end experience for a defined product area.
"owns the design direction and experience quality for your product vertical end to end"
Salary deliberately omitted — typical Revolut approach. You're in round 2 and in a strong position to ask. Don't wait for round 3.
"competitive compensation discussed at offer stage"
Fully remote confirmed — explicitly stated in the JD and confirmed during the first screening call. No surprise hybrid requirement.
Friction Signals
No salary stated — you're in round 2. This is the right moment to ask for a range before investing in a round 3. Don't skip this.
Fast-moving product culture may have high churn — Revolut's pace is well-documented. Confirm team stability and whether the previous Lead designer left or was promoted.
Questions to Ask
1
Can you share the salary range for this role ahead of a round 3 commitment?
2
Is this role a backfill? If so, what happened to the previous Lead Designer?
3
What does the design system and tooling infrastructure look like right now?

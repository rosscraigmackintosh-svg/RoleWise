Role Summary
analysis
Operator-Builder hybrid

This role sits at an interesting intersection: Stripe wants a PM who can run existing enterprise infrastructure like a business — with P&L awareness — while also having the product craft to shape complex B2B workflows. The contractor framing signals urgency and likely a backfill. At £650/day it prices fairly, but contract status means no equity upside on what could be a high-leverage seat. The main tension is whether they want a builder who can operate, or an operator who can build.

Fit Reality
4 signals
Your B2B SaaS and payments background maps well to Stripe's enterprise infrastructure problems.
"5+ years managing complex payment or financial infrastructure products in a B2B context"
Cross-functional leadership at scale is a genuine strength you can point to directly.
"Lead cross-functional squads across engineering, design, compliance, and finance stakeholders"
P&L ownership language is prominent — this may weight towards someone with formal financial responsibility.
"Owning the full P&L for this product line and presenting quarterly to CFO"
Contract framing limits strategic influence — you'd likely be executing against an existing roadmap, not setting direction.
What You'd Actually Do
day-to-day
1
Own the roadmap for Stripe's enterprise billing infrastructure — triaging between merchant pain points and internal platform needs.
2
Work closely with enterprise account managers and large merchants to translate commercial requirements into product specs.
3
Run quarterly business reviews with CFO-level stakeholders, presenting KPIs, revenue impact, and forward planning.
4
Coordinate releases across a multi-squad engineering team spanning infrastructure, frontend, and compliance.
Friction Signals
watch these
Contract with no mention of a conversion path — high risk of being a seat-warmer for a permanent hire they're already interviewing.
"Reporting into SVP of Product" suggests several management layers — immediate impact may be constrained to tactical execution.
No mention of discovery work or user research ownership — signals an execution-heavy role without upstream influence.
Questions Worth Asking
prep list
1
What does success look like at 90 days, and what does the permanent roadmap look like beyond the contract term?
2
Who owned this product before, and why is this a contract rather than a direct permanent hire?
3
How much of the P&L ownership is genuine decision-making authority vs. reporting and commentary?
4
What are the two or three biggest unresolved product debates on the team right now?
